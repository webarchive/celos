Major Research Projects

  • To set up a laser based flow cytometry and samples can be enzymes, antibodies or microbes. High Intensity UV LED for Time-gated Luminescence Flow Cytometer, under Prof. V.P.N. Nampoori at Centre of Excellence in Lasers and Optoelectronic Sciences.

In flow cytometry, naturally occurring, intrinsically fluorescent substances can severely compromise the unique discriminative ability of immune fluorescent probes. A novel time-gated luminescence flow cytometry (TGL FCM) design is presented that employs a rapid-pulsed ultra-violet (UV) light emitting diode (LED) and a time-gated channel photomultiplier tube (CPM). With this system, the micro europium luminescent microspheres were detected. The desired signal can be efficiently distinguished from the bulk autofluorescence when the fluorescence lifetime of the probe is sufficiently different from that of the autofluorescent matrix.

  • Infrared Spatial Interferomete, Space Sciences Laboratory, UC Berkeley working under Prof. C H Townes at Mt Wilson observatory. 
The ISI consists of three specially designed Pfund-type movable telescopes, each 1.65m in aperture.  By placing our three telescopes at varying distances apart from each other (ranging from 4m to 70m) and combining the starlight received from each one, we can obtain the same resolving power as a telescope as large as that separation in a perfect atmosphere.  This allows us to spatially resolve stars, which is otherwise impossible (for most stars) from the ground. The focous of the research is intend to imgaing the near by enviorment and the size of the red giant stars, including the complete opearation of the 3 ISI array telescopes located at Mt Wilson Observatory.
