Reading Section - Dimensional analysis, vectors and scalars, vector algebra, unit vectors, linearly independent and linearly dependent vectors, velocity, acceleration and force vectors

Module I - Motion along a straight line: velocity, acceleration, velocity –time graph, Newton’s laws of motion, equations of motion
Motion in two and three dimensions: projectiles, force, work and kinetic energy, energy conservation, work-kinetic energy theorem

Module II - Systems of particles: Newton’s laws for systems of particles, collisions, conservation of linear momentum, impulse, elastic and inelastic collisions in one and two dimensions
Rigid body dynamics: angular velocity and angular acceleration, angular momentum, torque, Newton’s laws for rotational motion, angular momenta for systems of particles, conservation of angular momentum, symmetry and laws of conservation (qualitative ideas)

Module III - Requirements of equilibrium, center of gravity, Newton’s laws of gravitation, gravitation near the surface of earth and inside the surface of earth, gravitational potential energy, central force, reduced mass, and Kepler’s laws

Module IV - Oscillations: simple harmonic motion, simple pendulum and its equation of motion, spring and spring constant, Hooke’s law, work done by a spring
Circular motion as simple harmonic motion, damped and forced oscillations, resonance

Module V - Waves: transverse and longitudinal waves, traveling and standing waves, energy and power in traveling waves, phase and group velocities, superposition, interference and dispersion of waves
Sound waves: traveling sound waves, intensity and sound levels, beats, Doppler Effect in sound and light, sound pollution

Advanced Reading Section - Acoustics of music, concert hall acoustics, mechanics of sports


References:
1. Fundamentals of Physics - Resnik, Halliday and Walker (Text)
2. Feynman Lectures Vol I
3. Classical Mechanics – Rana
