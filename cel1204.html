Module I - Frames of reference (basic ideas), mechanics of system of particles, conservative and neoconservative forces, conservation laws, constraints, constrained motion and constraint force, generalized coordinates calculus of Variation, Hamilton’s principle, Lagrange’s equations of motion

Module II - Lagrangian and Lagrange’s equations in simple cases like freely falling body, simple pendulum, harmonic oscillator
Lagrangian formulation of optics: optical Lagrangian, ray equation from Lagrange’s equation, rectilinear propagation and refraction of light

Module III - Conjugate momenta, cyclic coordinates, conservation theorems and symmetry, Noether’s theorem (no proof), Hamiltonian, relationship between Hamiltonian and Lagrangian, energy conservation

Module IV - Central force problem: reduction to equivalent one dimensional problem, Classification of orbits and stability condition for orbits, Kepler’s laws, Kepler’s second law as a consequence of angular momentum conservation

Module V - Hamilton’s equations of motions, canonical transformations, generating function, Poisson brackets, fundamental Poisson brackets, equations of motion using Poisson brackets, simple examples

Advanced Reading Section - Mathematical treatment of Kepler’s laws, Lagrangian formulation of normal modes and small oscillations as applied to CO2 molecules, action angle variables, Hamilton Jacobi equation, rigid body dynamics, Euler’s angles and equations of motion, symmetric top, Corioli’s force


References:
1. Classical Mechanics - Goldstein (Text)
2. Classical Mechanics - Rana
3. Modern Optics - R Guenther
4. Classical Mechanics - V B Bhatia
