Module I - Evolution of optical communication, evolution of fibre types, guiding properties of fibres, cross talk between fibres, coupled modes and mode mixing, dispersion properties of fibres, nonlinear properties of optical fibres, SRS, SBS, intensity dependent refractive index
Fibre design considerations: diameter, cladding, thickness, low and high bit rate systems, characterization of materials for fibres, fibre perform preparation, fibre drawing and control, roles of coating and jacketing

Module II - Optical and mechanical characterization of fibres, optical cable design: design objectives and cable structures, fibre splicing, fibre end preparation, single and array splices, measurement of splicing efficiency, optical fibre connectors, connector alignments, optical sources for communication, LED, injection lasers, modulation technique, direct and indirect methods, optical waveguide devices

Module III - Optical detectors, photodiodes in repeaters, receiver design, digital and analog , transmission system design, system design choices, passive and low speed active optical components for fibre system, micro-optic components, lens-less components, all fibre components

Module IV - Optical IC components for optical fibre components, electro optic devices for FO communication, optical switching, polarization control, inter office transmission system, trunking system, performance and architecture, under sea cable system, optical fibres in loop distribution system, photonic local network

Module V - Soliton communication: basic principle, metropolitan optical network, cable TV network, optical access network, photonics simulation tools, error control coding techniques, nonlinear optical effects in WDM transmission


References:
1. Optical Fibre Telecommunication - S E Miller, A G Chynoweth (Text)
2. Optical Fibre Telecommunication II - S E Miller, I Kaninov (Text)
3. Optical Fibre Telecommunication IV B - I Kaninov, T Li (Text)
4. Deploying Optical Network Components - Gil Held
