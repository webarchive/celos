Module I - Atoms, vector atom model, quantum numbers, atomic nucleus, relationship between nuclear radius and mass number, nuclear forces, nucleons, spin and isotopic spin, isotopes and isobars, isomers, mirror nuclei, stability of nuclei, alpha and beta decays, binding energy, fission and fusion
Nuclear models: semi empirical mass formula, liquid drop model, shell model, magic numbers, parity of nuclear states, meson theory of nuclear forces

Module II - Radio activity, units, radio activity, alpha and beta decay, Gamow’s theory, neutrino, Fermi’s theory of beta decay, radiation hazards, particle detectors, electroscope, scintillator, bubble chamber, cloud chamber, ionization chamber, GM counter, nuclear fusion and fission, source of stellar energy, thermonuclear reaction

Module III - Cosmic Rays: discovery, latitude, east west effect, altitude effect, primary and secondary cosmic rays, Cosmic ray showers, Bhabha’s theory, pair production and annihilation, positron and its discovery, discovery of pi and mu mesons, and strange particles, Van Allen belts, origin of cosmic rays, solar neutrino problem, neutrino oscillation and mass of neutrino

Module IV - Forces of nature and their unification (introductory ideas), nuclear reactions, conserved quantities in nuclear reactions, leptons, baryons, mesons and gauge particles, intrinsic and relative parity of elementary particles, Gellman-Nakano-Nishijima relation, fundamental particles and their classifications, parity violation and CPT conservation, CP violation and neutral Kaon decay, eightfold way, quark structure

Module V - Nuclear reactor: critical condition, design aspects, classification, breeder reactor, effect of nuclear radiation on living systems, nuclear reactors and environment protection
Particle accelerators: Van de Graaff generator, cyclotron, synchrotron, linear accelerator, colliders, atomic and ionic beams, ion implantation, isotopes and their applications
