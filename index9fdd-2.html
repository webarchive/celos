Reading section - Vector algebra and vector calculus

Module I - Electrostatics: electric field, Gauss’s law in integral and differential forms, applications of Gauss’s law, scalar potential, energy of a continuous charge distribution, Poisson and Laplace equations, boundary conditions and uniqueness theorem, dielectrics, induced dipoles, polarization and the field of a polarized object, Gauss’s law for dielectric media, displacement field, linear dielectrics and dielectric constant, energy and forces in dielectric systems
Magnetostatics: magnetic field and magnetic forces, Biot-Savart law, Ampere’s law, vector potential, magnetic susceptibility and permeability

Module II - Faraday’s law of electromagnetic induction, energy in magnetic field, Maxwell’s equations in vacuum and in dielectric media, vector and scalar potentials, guage transformations, Lorentz and Coulomb gauges, solution of Maxwell’s equations in vacuum and dielectric media, Poyinting theorem, conservation of energy and momentum, complex Poyinting vector

Module III - Reflection and refraction of EMW at dielectric boundaries, Snell’s law, TIR, Brewster angle, absorption and dispersion of EMW, skin effect, anomalous dispersion, wave packets, group velocity, metallic waveguides, Q-factor, radiating fields and antennas

Module IV - Michelson-Morley experiment, postulates of special theory of relativity, Lorentz transformations, velocity addition, velocity dependent mass, structure of space-time and Minkowski diagram

Module V - Relativistic mechanics, proper time and proper velocity, relativistic momentum and energy, Compton scattering, magnetism as a relativistic phenomenon, transformation of fields, field tensor, four vectors, Maxwell’s equations in tensor form

Advanced Reading Section - General theory of relativity, principle of equivalence, curvature of space time, curvature tensor, gravitation field


Reference:
1. Introduction to Electrodynamics - David Griffiths (Text)
2. Electrodynamics - J D Jackson
3. Feynman Lectures in Physics
